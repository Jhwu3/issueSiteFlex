Current Restoration Progress

In 1972, the clean water act prohibts regular dumping of waste and raw sweage into the New York Harbors. By 2000, water quality finally improves enough for life to survive in the harbor. In 2014, Billion Oyster Project launches with the goal to restore one billion oysters.

Billion Oyster Project is working closely with New York City through restaurants, schools and voluenteers. Students and teachers are involved though education, Voluenteer provide physical assistance with oyster restoration and restaurants are recycling oyster shells which provide the bed to grow new oysters.

Now in 2023, they have 18 active oyster restoration sites across 16 acres of New York Harbor, and have restored 100 million juvenile oysters since 2014. Oysters are starting to reproduce in the Harbor which is a sign that the population are becoming self-sustaining once again.
